13.8 B years ago
Cosmic Era
Scale: 1 second = 1 million years
🌌 The Cosmic Dawn
The universe begins
13.8 billion years ago
💥 The Big Bang
Space, time, and all matter burst into existence from an infinitely hot, dense point. The universe expands from a size smaller than an atom to cosmic proportions in fractions of a second.
13.8 billion years ago
✨ First Particles
Quarks, electrons, and other fundamental particles form in the first microsecond. Antimatter mostly annihilates with matter, leaving the small amount that makes up everything we see.
380,000 years ago
🌡️ First Light
The universe cools enough for atoms to form. Photons finally escape, creating the cosmic microwave background - the afterglow we can still detect today. This is the oldest light in the universe.
200 million years ago
🌑 First Stars
Gravity pulls hydrogen together to ignite the first stars. These massive, brilliant stars are made of pure hydrogen and helium - no heavier elements yet.
⭐ The Stellar Age
Galaxies form and stars burn
13 billion years ago
🌀 First Galaxies
Billions of stars cluster together to form the first galaxies. Our Milky Way is one of the oldest, forming around 13.2 billion years ago.
4.5 billion years ago
☀️ Sun is Born
A cloud of gas and dust collapses to form our Sun. The leftover material becomes the planets, moons, and asteroids of our solar system.
4.4 billion years ago
🌑 Moon Forms
A Mars-sized object called Theia crashes into Earth. Debris ejected into orbit slowly coalesces to form the Moon. The day was only 6 hours long!
🌍 Early Earth
A young, violent world
4.1 billion years ago
🌋 First Oceans
Comets and asteroids deliver water to Earth. Volcanic activity creates steam that rains down for millions of years, filling the first oceans.
3.9 billion years ago
☄️ Late Heavy Bombardment
Thousands of asteroids and comets rain down on the inner planets. The Moon's craters date from this violent period. Water and organic molecules arrive on Earth.
🧬 The Rise of Life
From simple cells to complex organisms
3.8 billion years ago
🦠 First Life
Simple, single-celled organisms appear - possibly near hydrothermal vents. The first life forms are bacteria and archaea, with no nucleus.
2.4 billion years ago
🫧 Great Oxidation Event
Cyanobacteria produce oxygen through photosynthesis. This poisons most existing life but creates the atmosphere we breathe. Iron in the oceans rusts, forming banded iron formations.
2 billion years ago
🧬 First Eukaryotes
Cells with a nucleus evolve. One cell engulfs another, creating mitochondria - the powerhouse of the cell. This endosymbiosis is the origin of complex life.
600 million years ago
🪼 Cambrian Explosion
Life diversifies dramatically in just a few million years. Eyes, shells, skeletons, and predators all evolve. Most animal phyla we know today appear suddenly in the fossil record.
500 million years ago
🐟 First Fish
The first vertebrates evolve. They're jawless and have no scales, but they are the ancestors of all fish, reptiles, birds, and mammals - including us.
🦖 Age of Dinosaurs
The reign of reptiles
230 million years ago
🦕 First Dinosaur
Small, bipedal reptiles evolve into the first dinosaurs. They start small but eventually dominate the planet for 160 million years.
150 million years ago
🦅 First Bird
Archaeopteryx evolves from small, feathered dinosaurs. Birds are living dinosaurs - they evolved from theropod ancestors.
66 million years ago
☄️ Extinction Event
An asteroid 10km wide strikes the Yucatan Peninsula. 75% of all species go extinct, including all non-avian dinosaurs. Mammals finally have their chance.
🧑 The Human Story
From apex predators to space explorers
6 million years ago
🦴 First Human Ancestor
Our ancestors split from chimps. The Sahara hasn't formed yet, and Africa is a lush paradise. We share 98.8% of our DNA with chimpanzees.
2 million years ago
🧥 Homo Erectus
Our ancestors stand upright, use tools, and control fire. They spread from Africa to Asia and Europe. Brain size doubles in just a few hundred thousand years.
300,000 years ago
🧠 Homo Sapiens Evolve
Modern humans appear in Africa. We have complex language, art, and the ability to think abstractly. We bury our dead with ceremony.
45,000 years ago
🎨 Cave Art
Humans create stunning cave paintings in Lascaux and Chauvet. These are the first known art, showing our need to express and document our world.
12,000 years ago
🌾 Agriculture Begins
Humans settle down and farm. Wheat, rice, and corn are domesticated. This triggers the birth of cities, civilization, and complex societies.
5,000 years ago
🏛️ First Civilizations
Sumer, Egypt, and the Indus Valley build the first cities. Writing, mathematics, and organized religion emerge. The first empires rise.
2,300 years ago
🔬 Scientific Revolution
Aristotle, Euclid, and others lay the foundations of logic, mathematics, and science. The method of reasoning that will transform humanity begins.
250 years ago
⚙️ Industrial Revolution
Steam engines transform society. Population explodes from 1 billion to 8 billion in just 200 years. We reshape the entire planet.
74 years ago
💻 Digital Age
Computers and the internet connect billions of people. Information travels at the speed of light. We can communicate instantly across the globe.
54 years ago
🌍 First Moon Landing
Humans walk on another world for the first time. "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." We finally see Earth from space.
🔮 The Future
What lies ahead
Today
📍 Present Day
8 billion humans live on Earth. We have mapped the human genome, discovered thousands of exoplanets, and sent robots to Mars. We are the only known intelligent life in the universe.
+100 years
🌡️ Climate Threshold
If emissions continue, global temperature rises 2-4°C. Sea levels rise, weather becomes extreme. The next century will define humanity's future.
+1,000 years
🧬 Post-Human Era
If we survive, we may become a multi-planetary species. Genetic engineering could eliminate disease. We might merge with AI or upload our consciousness.
+100,000 years
🚀 Interstellar Travel
Humanity could spread to other star systems. Generation ships or cryogenic sleep could take us to nearby stars. The first interstellar colonies are established.
+1 million years
🌌 Galactic Civilization
If we continue, humanity could span the galaxy. Millions of worlds. Trillions of humans. We would be fundamentally different from today's people.
+5 billion years
☀️ Sun Dies
The Sun runs out of hydrogen and expands into a red giant. Mercury, Venus, and probably Earth are consumed. Mars and the outer planets remain.
+100 billion years
💫 Last Stars
The last stars burn out. The universe becomes dark. Only brown dwarfs and black holes remain. The age of stars ends forever.
+10^100 years
🕳️ Heat Death
The universe reaches maximum entropy. All matter decays. Black holes evaporate over unimaginable timescales. The universe becomes cold, dark, and empty. Time loses all meaning.

🌌 The End... Or a New Beginning?

13.8 billion years of cosmic history led to conscious beings capable of understanding the universe. Every atom in your body was forged in stars. You are the universe experiencing itself.

What happens next is up to us.
